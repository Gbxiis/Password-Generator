Tips to ensure privacy and more secure passwords

  • Create phrases that are easy to remember and use the initials of each word to make up the password; to improve security, you can also exchange letters for numbers and similar characters. For example: a for @, l for 1, E for 3, and so on;
  • Mix letters, numbers and characters; this makes the password even more secure;
  • The longer the password, the harder it is to crack;
  • Don't use the same password on every website. This may sound complicated, but it lessens the chances that someone will have access to all your online data. One tip is to add information related to the specific website in your password - for example, include the initials FB, or FCB, or something similar in the middle of your password for Facebook;
  • The password should be difficult only for others! Always choose something that you can easily remember;
  • Never share your password! Sometimes, for reasons of trust or even naivety, we can end up revealing our password to someone else - such as a friend, girlfriend, boyfriend or girlfriend.
  • Avoid writing the password down somewhere. The ideal thing is to memorize it. If you can't, try to write it down in a code you understand and hide it well on the paper it's on;
  • When you leave a website, always remember to log out. That way, if someone else uses the computer after you, they are less likely to find your account open.
